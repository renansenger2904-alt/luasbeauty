What It Is

Smoother skin, in one hour.

Dermaplaning is a precision exfoliation technique performed with a sterile, single-use surgical-grade blade — gently removing the outermost layer of dead cells and the fine vellus hair (peach fuzz) that sits on the surface of the skin. The result is immediate: skin that looks brighter, feels remarkably soft, and reflects light beautifully — with makeup applying more smoothly than it has in months.

— Why it works

Removing the layer that dulls everything else.

Dead skin cells and vellus hair sit on the surface of your skin every day — diffusing light, blocking the absorption of your serums and creams, and creating an uneven base for makeup. Dermaplaning lifts that entire layer away, in a single, careful pass.

What's revealed underneath is the skin you forgot you had: brighter, softer, and ready to drink in the actives that follow. Many clients tell us their products feel twice as effective after dermaplaning.

Common Questions

Things worth knowing.

Honest answers to the questions our North Perth clients ask most.

Will the hair grow back darker or thicker?
No — this is the most common myth. Vellus hair grows back at exactly the same rate, texture and colour. Dermaplaning does not affect the hair follicle in any way.
How often should I have dermaplaning?
Every 4 to 6 weeks is ideal — that aligns with the natural skin renewal cycle. Many clients book it as their monthly ritual.
Does it hurt?
Not at all — most clients describe it as relaxing. There's a light scratching sensation but no pain. The blade is incredibly fine and never digs into the skin.
Is there any downtime?
None. You'll leave looking glowing — and many clients schedule it before events for exactly this reason. The only aftercare is good sun protection.
Can I combine dermaplaning with other treatments?
Absolutely. Dermaplaning Glow already includes a full facial. For added benefit, many clients pair it with LED Light Therapy or upgrade to a Hydra Oxygen Facial after.
